The three root causes

⚠ Root Cause 1 — Information Asymmetry Farmer doesn't know their own beans' SCA score, defect profile, or cupping notes. Co-op buyer does (or claims to). Without verifiable quality data, farmer is a price-taker. This is what computer vision solves.
⚠ Root Cause 2 — No Direct Channel Even a high-scoring farmer can't reach a Tokyo or Berlin roaster. Export bureaucracy, cross-border payment, language barriers, and trust deficits make direct trade impossible without infrastructure. This is what the marketplace solves.
⚠ Root Cause 3 — Wrong Crop in 5 Years Climate is shifting growing zones uphill at ~150m per decade. Farmer planting Caturra today at 1,200m will harvest collapsed yield in 2035. Government extension services don't have the data or tools. This is what the variety advisor solves.
📊 Important Context — Why Now Three things make 2026 the right year: (1) Smartphone penetration in highland villages just crossed 80% — every Akha kid has Android Go. (2) Stripe Connect + cross-border PromptPay rails matured. (3) Hill tribe citizenship process accelerated June 2025, removing a major friction in farmer banking and export documentation. The infrastructure for a direct-trade app exists for the first time.

SECTION 04Why Merging Wins

Three compounding reasons: lifecycle integration, data flywheel, and moat layering.

1. Lifecycle integration

A farmer who used your variety advisor in March will have your app on their phone in November when harvest comes. Conversion to the scanner module is essentially free. Reverse is also true: a farmer impressed by their first scan score wants to know which variety would have scored higher. One install, two products, zero re-acquisition cost.

2. Data flywheel

This is the one most teams miss. Each module's data improves the others:

  • Variety + GPS + harvest score: over 2 seasons you build the world's first dataset linking Thai elevation/microclimate to actual cupping outcomes. No coffee research org has this. It becomes the recommendation engine's training data.
  • Defect scans + processing method + final price: trains a "process recommender" — given your bean defects + weather, should you do washed, honey, or natural to maximize price?
  • Roaster preferences + farmer profiles: matches improve over time. After 1,000 transactions you can predict which roaster will love which farmer's lot before either knows.
📊 Important Context — Data Moat Math Each scan = one labeled training row. Hit 10,000 farmers averaging 5 scans/season = 50,000 Thai-specific labeled green-bean photos annually. Public SCA datasets have ~5,000 photos total, mostly African/Latin American beans. After year 1 your model is strictly better at Thai beans than anything Cropster, Bext360, or BeanGrader can build remotely. That gap widens every year.

3. Moat layering

Two products = two competitors per slice. One integrated app = competitors must build all six modules to displace you. The cost of replacement grows faster than the cost of incremental features.

The counterfactual: what if you don't merge?

If you ship #7 and #8 separately, here's what happens within 18 months:

  • #7 alone gets cloned by an Indonesian or Vietnamese team within 6 months. CV defect detection is a published technique now.
  • #8 alone struggles with retention — farmers open it once a year, can't afford a subscription, churn is brutal.
  • Together they reinforce each other and become a category-defining product. The combined version is 10× harder to clone.

SECTION 07Product — Six Modules, Deconstructed

Each module solves one stage of the lifecycle. Together they form an inseparable whole.

1
Pre-plant · Annual

Site & Variety Advisor

Farmer drops a GPS pin on their plot. App pulls elevation, slope, aspect, soil type, climate history, and IPCC RCP4.5 climate projections. Output: ranked variety recommendations with explanations the farmer understands.

Inputs
GPS lat/lng → DEM elevation → slope/aspect → LDD soil DB → TMD historical 20-yr climate → IPCC scenarios for 2030/2040
Output
"Plot at 1,420m, NE-facing, 22°C avg. Recommend Catimor 70% + Typica 30%. By 2035, pure Caturra unviable — hedge by planting 200m lower."
📊 Why this matters A coffee tree takes 3-4 years to first harvest, 5+ to peak. A wrong variety choice today is a 7-year economic mistake. No farmer has access to this combined dataset; government extension officers don't either. This module is essentially the only consumer-facing tool that synthesizes climate science for highland farmers.
2
Growing season · Weekly

Disease & Pest Scout

Farmer photographs a suspect leaf. On-device CV classifies common Thai coffee threats: leaf rust (roya), coffee berry borer, coffee berry disease, anthracnose. Hyperlocal outbreak map shows what other farmers in the watershed are reporting.

Tech
TFLite vision model, 4MB, runs offline. Trained on PlantVillage + Thai-specific data we collect through field partnerships.
Treatment advice
Organic-cert friendly recommendations (matters for export premium). Links to nearest agri-shop with stock.
3
Harvest window · Daily during season

Harvest Timer

Cherry photo → ripeness percentage via color/firmness ML. Combined with 7-day weather forecast → "Pick Tuesday, not today; rain Thursday will dilute Brix sugars." Then the process recommender suggests washed/honey/natural based on your bean profile, weather window, and current market premiums.

4
★ HERO MODULE · Post-harvest

Bean Quality Scanner

Spread 50-100 green beans on white A4 paper (free kit shipped to farmer). Phone camera captures. MobileNetV3 fine-tuned on SCA defect taxonomy classifies every visible bean.

SCA Score Estimate: 84.5 ✓ Specialty Grade Cat-1 Defects: 0 ✓ Cat-2 Defects: 3 (2 partial sour, 1 floater) Bean Size: Screen 17 (87%), Screen 16 (13%) Predicted Cupping: floral, jasmine, citrus acidity Suggested Roast: light to medium-light Price Floor (suggested): ฿1,400/kg green
⚠ Legal & Trust Boundary Output is explicitly labeled "pre-screening estimate" — not a Q-grader certification. For commercial export, lots above 80 SCA route to a certified Q-grader (in-app, paid). This protects against legal liability and maintains trust with the specialty community. Underclaiming credentials is a moat, not a weakness.
5
★ HERO MODULE · Sell

Direct-to-Roaster Marketplace

Each farmer's harvest becomes a lot card: photos, story video, AI cupping report, processing detail, organic/fair-trade certs, available kg. Roasters worldwide browse, request samples, negotiate, settle in escrow. Stripe Connect handles cross-border. Initial export logistics partnered with Beanspire or This Side Up.

💰 Pricing Intelligence Layer Cross-references the lot against recent global comparables: "Similar 84.5-score Catimor honey from Aceh sold to Onyx for £14/kg green last month. Recommend asking £11-13." This is the killer feature — a farmer who doesn't speak English now negotiates like an experienced exporter.
6
Marketing · Continuous

Farmer Storyteller

Farmer records a 30-second voice note in Akha, Hmong, Karen, or Northern Thai. Claude translates and reframes into English / Mandarin / Japanese / Korean marketing copy that captures the cultural authenticity buyers crave. Auto-generates a vertical reel with farmer's face + farm B-roll for IG/TikTok.

★ Why this completes the moat Direct trade isn't just about price discovery — it's about relationship and story. Specialty coffee buyers pay a premium for narrative provenance ("this lot from a 4th-gen Akha family"). By owning storytelling, you build farmer brand independent of the platform. Counterintuitive but critical: farmers will trust a platform more if they see their personal brand outliving their account on it.

SECTION 08Tech Stack — Each Decision Justified

Defensible technical choices, with reasoning a senior engineer can challenge.

LayerChoiceWhy this, not alternative
Mobile framework Flutter Single codebase, runs on Android Go (cheap phones farmers actually own). Better native performance than React Native for camera + ML inference. Hot reload speeds hackathon iteration.
On-device ML TFLite, MobileNetV3 quantized 4MB model, runs offline. Critical because farms have intermittent 4G. Quantization to int8 keeps accuracy within 2% of float32 but cuts size 4×.
Defect detector Transfer-learn from ImageNet → fine-tune 500 hand-labeled Thai green-bean photos + 5,000 public SCA dataset is enough for >90% accuracy. Avoids expensive cold-start data collection.
Variety recommender XGBoost (not deep learning) Tabular data, small feature set, need explainability for farmer trust. SHAP values let us show "this variety because elevation + your soil pH + projected 2035 temperature." Black-box model would not be trusted.
Backend Node + Postgres + S3 Boring tech that works. Hackathon timeline doesn't reward novelty in plumbing. Postgres handles the marketplace, S3 stores scan photos for retraining.
LLM Claude Haiku 4.5 Cheap (~$0.25/1M input tokens), fast, exceptional Thai language quality, handles minority languages (Akha/Hmong) better than GPT for low-resource Asian languages. Cache farmer profile prompts for further cost reduction.
Marketplace payments Stripe Connect Cross-border escrow built-in. Handles forex, KYC, tax forms. Without this, an MVP cross-border marketplace is 6 months of work; with it, 2 weeks.
Maps + geo Mapbox + Thailand DOA soil layer Free tier covers MVP. Mapbox style customization lets us render highland topography clearly (Google Maps washes out elevation detail).
📊 The offline-first decision (most important tech choice) Doi Chang has 4G in 60% of plots, none in 40%. If the scanner needs internet, the app dies in the field. Every core function must work offline: scan, variety advice (cached for the farmer's location), pest ID, farmer story drafting (deferred LLM call). Marketplace browsing is the only online-required feature. This single architectural choice — designing for the worst connectivity in the user base — is what distinguishes "tech for farmers" from "tech tourism."

SECTION 10Business Model — Three Revenue Streams

Diversified revenue. No single channel can kill the business.

Stream 1: Farmer freemium SaaS (B2C)

  • Free tier: 5 scans/month, basic variety advice, community access
  • Pro tier (99฿/mo): unlimited scans, market analytics, lot listing priority, video story generation, multi-language

Conversion target: 30% free→Pro after first successful sale. Justification: a single direct-trade sale pays for ~2 years of Pro.

Stream 2: Roaster subscription + take rate (B2B)

  • Browse free: see lots, contact farmers, no commitment
  • Source 990฿/mo: API access, automatic sourcing alerts, RFP for bulk orders
  • Transaction fee: 8% on closed deals. Industry standard for marketplaces of this thinness.

Stream 3: Government & foundation partnership (B2G)

  • Onboarding fee: Doi Tung Foundation, DOAE, TCEB pay 200฿ per farmer onboarded for impact reporting
  • Carbon credit verification: shade-grown coffee qualifies for voluntary carbon markets — separate revenue line, potentially $50-200 per farm/year
  • Climate adaptation grants: ม.แม่ฟ้าหลวง / TSRI fund the variety advisor research extension
💰 Unit Economics (Year 2 cohort) Farmer CAC: 200฿ via Doi Tung village ambassadors (paid per onboard).
Farmer LTV: 99฿/mo × 24 mo retention × 30% Pro = 712฿. LTV/CAC ≈ 3.5× — healthy for vertical SaaS.
Plus marketplace: avg farmer does 2 transactions/yr × $1,000 avg × 8% × 2 years = $320 incremental revenue per Pro farmer. Combined LTV approaches $350.
Roaster LTV/CAC: 990฿/mo × 36 mo + transaction fees ≈ ฿80,000 against 3,000฿ digital marketing CAC. ~25× ratio.

SECTION 12Five Layers of Defensible Moat

Each layer is independent. A competitor must crack all five to displace you.

01
Thai-specific data flywheel
Every scan trains a defect detector specifically tuned for Catimor + Typica + Geisha grown in Doi Chang volcanic soil. Foreign datasets (mostly Brazilian, Ethiopian) underperform on Thai beans. Year-1 advantage compounds annually.
02
Hill tribe trust through Foundation channel
Doi Tung Foundation partnership is not replicable. Royal Thai legacy, 30+ years of community work. Foreign players cannot get this access. Without trust, farmers won't adopt — and trust takes decades to build.
03
Multi-language voice + storytelling
Akha, Hmong, Karen, Northern Thai (kham mueang), Lao — five low-resource languages crucial to the user base. No global SaaS will localize this. Building in-house gives a 3-5 year head start that no general LLM company addresses.
04
Lifecycle integration lock-in
Once a farmer's variety choice, harvest data, and roaster relationships live in DoiBean, switching costs become punitive. They'd lose multi-season history. Roasters similarly invested in lot reviews, sample history.
05
Two-sided network effects
More farmers → more lots → attracts more roasters. More roasters → higher prices realized → attracts more farmers. Classic chicken-and-egg, but Doi Tung partnership cracks it from the supply side first.

SECTION 13Risks & Mitigations

Every honest pitch lists what could kill it.

Old farmers don't use apps
HIGH
Voice-first UI, paid youth ambassadors per village, partner with Doi Tung's existing channels. Training program with grandkids onboarding grandparents.
Photo quality variance
MED
Free A4 white scan kit shipped on signup. In-app camera guide with real-time feedback. Train CV model on intentionally noisy data (poor lighting, partial occlusion).
Roasters distrust remote scoring
MED
Sample escrow with return guarantee. Q-grader certification on premium lots (paid in-app). First 100 transactions backstopped by DoiBean reputation.
Cupping prediction is hard from vision alone
MED
Position as "estimate" not "score". Premium tier offers $50 NIR sensor add-on for chemistry-based prediction. Partner with Q-graders for top lots.
Cross-border export bureaucracy
HIGH
First 100 transactions handled by partnered exporters (Beanspire, This Side Up). Build in-house export ops only after volume justifies. Hire compliance specialist year 2.
Cropster or Bext360 enters Thailand
LOW
By the time a global player decides Thailand is worth localizing, our data flywheel + foundation trust is years deep. Buy-vs-build for them favors acquisition.
PM2.5 burning season disrupts farms
MED
Counterintuitive — fires push farmers to value-added income (specialty premium) over volume. Aligns with our pitch: "your only escape from burn-cycle income is premium pricing."
LLM hallucinates farmer story / mistranslates
LOW
Always show original voice + AI translation side-by-side. Farmer approves before publish. Native speaker review for first 1,000 listings (use ambassador network).

SECTION 1560-Day MVP Plan

Build only what proves the thesis. Cut everything else.

Week 1–2
Defect detection model

Fine-tune MobileNetV3 on 500 hand-labeled Thai green-bean photos + 5,000 public SCA dataset. Target: 85%+ accuracy on Cat-1 detection. Quantize to int8, package for TFLite.

Flutter shell + offline scan

Week 7–8

Week 9
Demo polish + pitch deck
